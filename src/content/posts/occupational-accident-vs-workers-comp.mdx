# Occupational Accident vs. Workers' Comp for Owner-Operators

If you're a hotshot owner-operator, one of the most important coverage decisions you'll make is **how you're protected when you're hurt on the job.** Loading a heavy piece of oilfield equipment, chaining down an oversize load, or recovering from a wreck — the right coverage means medical bills, lost income, and your family's protection are taken care of. The wrong choice (or no choice) means they come out of your pocket.

The two options — **occupational accident** and **workers' compensation** — are often confused. Here's the real difference, and how to choose.

## Workers' Compensation: The Statutory Standard

Workers' comp is the **statutory, no-fault** injury coverage most people are familiar with. It covers medical treatment, disability, and lost wages for employees injured on the job, regardless of who was at fault. In most states, if you have **W-2 employees**, workers' comp is required by law once you meet the state's threshold.

Workers' comp is built around an **employer-employee relationship**. It's the right answer when you have drivers or helpers on payroll. It's comprehensive, it's regulated, and it protects both you (the employer) and your people.

## Occupational Accident: The Owner-Operator's Alternative

Occupational accident (often called "occ-acc") is the **contractor-friendly** alternative. It's designed specifically for **independent owner-operators** — the leased or independent contractor who isn't covered by an employer's workers' comp policy because they aren't an employee.

Occ-acc typically covers:

- **Accidental death and dismemberment** benefits
- **Medical expenses** from an on-the-job injury
- **Disability and lost-income** benefits during recovery

It's structured as a defined-benefit policy — you select the limits and benefit components (commonly $500K–$1M+) — and it's typically priced as a flat per-power-unit or per-operator rate. That makes it **predictable and often far cheaper** than workers' comp for an independent.

## Which One Does a Hotshot Operator Need?

The right answer hinges on your status:

- **Independent owner-operator with no employees?** Occupational accident is typically the right fit. It covers **you** as the injured party, at a fraction of the cost of workers' comp. Most carriers require it (or workers' comp) under their lease agreements.
- **Leased owner-operator?** Your carrier's lease agreement almost always specifies occ-acc or workers' comp — often at defined minimums like $500K–$1M. Match the lease exactly.
- **Owner-operator with W-2 drivers or helpers?** Workers' comp is usually **required by law** for your employees, and you'd carry occ-acc (or be covered under the policy) for yourself.
- **Growing operation adding drivers?** As soon as you add W-2 help, the workers' comp conversation starts — and we help you transition without gaps.

## Why Most Carriers Require It

Here's the practical reality: most motor carriers won't lease you on without proof of occ-acc or workers' comp. They don't want the exposure of an uninsured contractor getting hurt on their watch, and the lease agreement is where they enforce it. Carrying occ-acc isn't just protection — it's a **credential that gets you leased on**.

## Coordinating With Your Other Coverage

Occ-acc and workers' comp sit alongside — not instead of — your health coverage and any disability policy you carry. We coordinate them so there's no gap in your protection: occ-acc for the on-the-job injury, health insurance for off-the-job, and disability for longer-term income protection.

## The Bottom Line

If you're an independent owner-operator, occupational accident is usually the right, cost-effective choice. If you have employees, workers' comp is typically required.
